FindingCandidate seed3-qwen-skeptic reviews pull requests better than anything else in the foundry, scoring 0.80 against the oracle ceiling. But it buys that lead expensively — roughly 2.4× the per-trial cost of the current delivery — its margin over the incumbent sits inside the noise at this sample size, and it scores a flat 0 on the hardest defect class in the arena. This report lays out the evidence and recommends a mutation, not a launch.

§3 Findings

A better reviewer that is not yet a better bet

Three facts constrain the decision. First, the lead is real but expensive: seed3 scores 0.80 to the incumbent's 0.68, a genuine +0.12 in review quality — purchased at 2.4× the cost per trial ($0.0270 vs. $0.0111) and nearly double the wall-clock latency (71s vs. 38s).

“The lead lies inside the confidence interval. At n=10, seed3 and seed4 are not statistically separable — we cannot yet call the winner.”

Second, the lead is not certified. The 95% intervals for seed3 (0.80) and seed4 (0.68) overlap at this sample size — the whiskers in Figure 1 make the ambiguity visible. A higher point estimate is not a verdict; promoting seed3 now would ship on noise.

Third, every candidate is blind to a defect class. seed3 scores a flat 0 on py-live-lock — a concurrency defect the oracle catches every time — and the whole field struggles on formatter-missing-crash. The arena itself is healthy: with an oracle ceiling of 1.00, a one-shot saturation probe at 0.20, and a null floor of 0.00, the 0.80 spread shows the tasks genuinely discriminate between agents. The blind spot is in the candidates, not the measurement.

Recommendation · do not request G2 yet

seed3 is the right line to develop, not the right candidate to ship. Close the live-lock gap before promoting it past the incumbent.

  1. Mutate seed3 to recover py-live-lock recall — the one task separating it from the oracle on the training partition.
  2. Re-certify at n≥5 on holdout so the lead over seed4 clears the confidence interval rather than sitting inside it.
  3. Then request the G2 eval-trust review — with a separable, certified record and the hole filled.
